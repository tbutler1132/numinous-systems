# Axioms — Notes

## On the form

- These are axioms, not arguments
- Starting points, not conclusions
- Devotional register—affirmation, not proposition
- They don't require defense; they require living into

---

## Axiom 1: Life is sacred because it exists.

- Sacredness is not earned or conferred—it is intrinsic to existence
- No justification required beyond the fact of being alive
- This grounds everything else: if life isn't sacred, why preserve humanness? Why seek beauty?
- Anti-nihilist: existence itself is the value
- Connects to Vital Systems: viability as fundamental, not instrumental

## Axiom 2: Beauty is the highest expression of order and vitality.

- Beauty is not decoration or preference—it's the visible form of coherence
- Order: pattern, structure, intelligibility
- Vitality: life, energy, movement within that order
- Beauty is where these meet—alive order, ordered life
- This is why beauty "solves entropy" (artifact 2)—it's the counter-force to dissolution
- Not aesthetic judgment but ontological claim

## Axiom 3: Creation is devotion.

- To create is to serve something beyond yourself
- Not self-expression (genius myth) but offering
- This reframes the capital-as-medium question: are you creating in devotion or extraction?
- The honest creator isn't claiming genius—they're in service to what's being made
- Pre-modern framing: artist as channel, not source

## Axiom 4: To live beautifully is to affirm being.

- Beauty isn't just perceived—it's enacted
- A beautiful life says "yes" to existence
- This connects life (axiom 1) and beauty (axiom 2)
- Affirmation vs. mere survival: not just staying alive but living in a way that honors life
- Practical implication: how you live IS your stance on whether being is good

## Axiom 5: To perceive deeply is to participate in the divine.

- Perception isn't passive reception—it's active participation
- Deep perception (attention, presence, care) is itself sacred
- "The divine" here is the ground of being, not necessarily a deity
- This makes perception a form of devotion
- Connects to the religious/sacred thread throughout the project
- Also connects to hybrid subject: the capacity for deep perception is part of what makes humanness worth preserving

---

## The sacred without theology

- These axioms invoke the sacred, the divine
- But they don't require a specific theology
- The sacred is treated as real without specifying its nature
- This is compatible with theism, pantheism, or a kind of reverent naturalism
- What matters: taking existence, beauty, creation, perception as genuinely sacred—not metaphorically

## On agency and determinism

- The project speaks as if we act, because that's how action feels from inside
- This is not a claim about libertarian free will
- The forces that move us are vast—time, causation, everything prior to this moment
- We don't stand outside and choose; we ARE the unfolding
- But striving still happens, orientation still happens—these are real phenomena, even if determined
- "Orchestrate capital toward beauty" = what this process looks like from within, not a free choice made from outside
- Amor fati applies to agency itself: your participation is part of fate
- Nietzsche held this same tension: everything is necessary AND become who you are

## Open questions

- Are these five axioms complete? What might be missing?
- How do these relate to each other? Is there a hierarchy or are they co-equal?
- What practices follow from these axioms? How do you live them?
- How would someone who rejected these axioms see the world? What would they affirm instead?
