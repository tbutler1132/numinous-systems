# Love of Fate

## Artifact Concept

This folder contains a developing essay on **love of fate** (*amor fati*)—as a necessary posture toward existence.

The core claim: regret is the enemy. Not because loss doesn't matter, but because the thought "if only..." infects and destroys everything it touches. You cannot fully inhabit the life you have while holding open a comparison to the life you didn't get.

Acceptance isn't resignation—it's the prerequisite for presence.

## What This Artifact Addresses

- Why regret (not loss itself) is the enemy
- The difference between love of fate and positive thinking, rationalization, or resignation
- How regret fractures presence and blocks the experience of beauty
- The logic: the past cannot be changed, so comparing to an unchangeable alternative is pure loss
- What closing the question and affirming what is actually means

## Connection to Broader Project

This artifact makes explicit a posture that runs through the other essays:

- **Axioms**: "To live beautifully is to affirm being." Love of fate is this axiom applied to loss.
- **Beauty Redeems**: Beauty freezes time—the moment where everything is enough. Regret keeps time open as perpetual lack.
- **The Living System**: The viable range requires acceptance of the hybrid condition. You navigate what is.
- **Capital as Medium**: You cannot optimize your past. Navigate from where you are.
